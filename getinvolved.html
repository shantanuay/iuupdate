Get involved with India Untravelled

At India Untravelled, we are always on the lookout for volunteers who are passionate about travel and interested in contributing their skills and creativity to further the cause of rural and responsible travel in India. You can browse through our on-going and upcoming projects below, and use the Volunteer Form (at the bottom of this page) to submit your application.

Please note:

  • All our projects are location independent and can be worked on from home.
  • We expect our volunteers to be self-starters and own the project(s) they work on.
  • You can volunteer on a project-basis or for a minimum period of one month, unless otherwise mutually agreed upon.
  • If you have any volunteering ideas not included below, please indicate them in your application.
  • Please allow us 7-10 days to respond to your volunteer application.
  • At the end of your volunteering stint, we'll be happy to write you a recommendation (on LinkedIn or otherwise, subject to the quality of your work).

ROAD WARRIOR:

If you are checking in from the road, or are exceptionally passionate about a particular state or kind of experience in India, you can be our road warrior and help us source new partners for India Untravelled. Our ideal partners are village communities, families, organizations and individuals who provide socially and/or environmentally responsible travel alternatives in less travelled parts of India, and could benefit from our online marketing efforts. We personally handpick each of our partners, and your recommendations will help us establish partnerships along more roads untravelled.


TRAVEL RESEARCHER:

We are in search of extra pairs of eyes, to watch the travels space for us, curate travel lists for our blog and social media channels, and scout new opportunities for us to showcase India Untravelled. If Google is your best friend, we could use your help in scaling our marketing efforts further.


CREATIVE GENIUS:

Our enterprise is reliant on our ability to take offline travel experiences and showcase them online. There is no end to the creativity we can use to inspire travellers to travel responsibly, set off the beaten path, and discover the spirit of India. If you want to experiment with your creative juices, you can volunteer in the following ways:

  • Making videos / short films: We'll provide photographs, written material and testimonials of past travellers to help you conceptualize and create destination-specific videos as well as short films and campaigns on responsible travel.
  • Designing marketing material: If photoshop is your thing, you can help us design postcards, posters and other marketing material that can be used in our online and offline campaigns, as well as on our Facebook page and Pinterest boards.
  • Online Campaigns: You can help us brainstorm for new low-budget campaign ideas related to rural and responsible travel, as well as take the lead to execute these campaigns using social media tools.

TWITTER AMBASSADOR:

We seek to take our responsible travel message to the Twitterverse more actively in 2013, as well as showcase our partner destinations to potential travellers. If you're a Twitter addict, you can volunteer in the following ways:

  • Hosting an #Untravel chat: In 2012, we partnered with The Alternative to host a Twitter chat every Friday afternoon, discussing topics ranging from road trips to solo travelling. This year, we seek to involve volunteers to represent @IndiaUntraveled in hosting and leading some of the weekly chats..
  • Tweeting as @IndiaUntraveled: We are looking for more crowd sourced tweeting and interaction on our Twitter profile (on topics relevant to our work), for which we seek volunteers familiar with Twitter to tweet from the @IndiaUntraveled handle; your commitment could vary from one to a few hours a week. Gradually, we want you to help us shape an on-going Twitter Ambassador program.

SOCIAL MEDIA ADDICT:

In an attempt to grow our online reach, we have set up our accounts on Flickr, Pinterest, Slideshare and YouTube. You can assist us in managing one or more of these channels, by sourcing and adding content, making it searchable, and interacting on our behalf with active users and influencers. It's all fun if social media is your thing!


RESIDENT BLOGGER:

The India Untravelled blog is a platform for amateur bloggers and writers to share travel experiences from around India. You can take the lead as our resident blogger and blog manager, and create content for us as well as help us source guest bloggers who would like to contribute their stories. You can also propose blogging-centered campaigns to strengthen responsible travel or India-specific resources online.
